**Standing lessons (carry forward):**
- **pass@k (k‚Č•4, n‚Č•8) is the metric for *collapse/regression*** ‚ÄĒ greedy pass@1 (¬Ī2/25 noise) and the SDPO loss are both blind to it.
- **‚Ä¶but pass@8 SATURATES, so it's the wrong metric for *improvement* on solvable problems.** At n=12 it's bimodal ‚ÄĒ on the iter-08 probe pool **29 problems = 0, 26 = 1.0, only 4 in between**; *every* trained-frontier problem has base pass@8 = 1.0, so pass@8 **cannot** rise there (this is why iter-05‚Üí08 saw nothing). For gains, measure **mean *sampled* pass@1** (resolution 1/n, spreads problems across 0.08‚Äď0.83) ‚ÄĒ it's what SDPO moves (distilling pass@k success raises the floor toward the ceiling). *Sampled-mean* pass@1 (n‚Č•16, temp>0, bootstrap CI) ‚Č† the *greedy single-shot* pass@1 the line above warns against. Pick the metric by what you're detecting: pass@k for collapse, sampled pass@1 for improvement.
- **Select training data by measured solvability** (learnability frontier), not difficulty label.
- SDPO on the frontier = **capability elicitation/sharpening** (pass@k‚Üípass@1), bounded by base pass@k; new capability (hard) needs **live judge feedback** (iteration 2).
- **Select the frontier band by BINARY solve rate at the TRAINING temperature** (not a dense/coarse probe). The lever is *data selection* (which problems split under the binary advantage) ‚ÄĒ NOT dense reward (a distinct, gaming-prone alternative). iter-07's band (dense score, n=4 probe) was 69% flat-under-binary ‚Üí `flat_group` 0.44; iter-08's n=12 binary re-probe (temp 1.0) ‚Üí 10 problems ‚Üí `flat_group` 0.12. Flat probability is `p‚Āł+(1‚ąíp)‚Āł`, minimized at p‚Čą0.5; keep `p‚ąą[~0.25,0.75]`.
- **Mode collapse here = a self-distillation diversity loss** (pass@k erodes; "length-held ‚Č† diversity-held" ‚ÄĒ pass@k is the metric, not length): flat reward groups ‚Üí weak policy gradient ‚Üí self-distillation sharpens. The **mechanism** is confirmed: frontier-band data revives the GRPO policy gradient (`flat_group` 0.75‚Üí0.44‚Üí0.12 across iter-06/07/08, a direct per-step measurement). **BUT reviving the gradient did NOT produce a measurable pass@8 gain** ‚ÄĒ the iter-08 definitive eval (30 problems, n=12, bootstrap CI) found ALL iterations' CIs overlap base. The earlier "+0.083 reversed the collapse, first iter to beat base" claim was **12-probe noise and is retracted**. **iter-09+10 CLOSED the open question:** applying the revived gradient does NOT buy capability at any dose. iter-09 (LR 1e-4, őĒW 2.40) ‚Üí full collapse (pass@1 ‚ąí0.20, length 16k‚Üí5.5k); iter-10 (LR 5e-5, őĒW 1.34) ‚Üí *milder* collapse (pass@1 0.29, length 16k‚Üí8k); iter-08 (3e-5) ‚Üí null (length held, no gain). **The collapse is DOSE-DEPENDENT and monotonic (more dose ‚Üí more collapse ‚Üí lower pass@1); there is NO goldilocks LR ‚ÄĒ the only non-collapsing dose is too small to help, and no dose beats base.** So the collapse is the SDPO self-distillation *direction* (brevity); LR only sets its speed/floor. The fix is a **direction lever** (entropy/length guard, or KL ‚ÄĒ but `--beta` is inert in SDPOTrainer, `sdpo-beta-inert`), NOT LR tuning.
- **Never let a small-probe őĒ drive an iteration; gate every capability claim on a CI that excludes zero.** The whole iter-06/07/08 "collapse‚Üífix" arc rested on a 12-problem √ó n=8 probe whose ¬Ī0.15 noise fabricated a ¬Ī0.25 "monotonic trend" that vanished on a 30-problem n=12 bootstrap-CI eval. A powered eval (‚Č•30 problems, n‚Č•12, bootstrap CI) run *once, early* is cheap insurance against chasing noise. pass@8 point estimates from <~25 problems are not a basis for any conclusion.
- **The 8B thinking-ON SDPO regime needs Modal H200 (‚Č•141GB) ‚ÄĒ the GB10's 128GB unified memory OOM-cascades on the training backward** (full-vocab logits over ~20k-token sequences); the 512-cap smoke does NOT exercise that peak. GB10 stays the box for cheap diagnostics/eval/plotting.
