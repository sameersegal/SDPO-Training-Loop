**Standing lessons (carry forward):**
- **pass@k (k≥4, n≥8) is the metric for *collapse/regression*** — greedy pass@1 (±2/25 noise) and the SDPO loss are both blind to it.
- **…but pass@8 SATURATES, so it's the wrong metric for *improvement* on solvable problems.** At n=12 it's bimodal — on the iter-08 probe pool **29 problems = 0, 26 = 1.0, only 4 in between**; *every* trained-frontier problem has base pass@8 = 1.0, so pass@8 **cannot** rise there (this is why iter-05→08 saw nothing). For gains, measure **mean *sampled* pass@1** (resolution 1/n, spreads problems across 0.08–0.83) — it's what SDPO moves (distilling pass@k success raises the floor toward the ceiling). *Sampled-mean* pass@1 (n≥16, temp>0, bootstrap CI) ≠ the *greedy single-shot* pass@1 the line above warns against. Pick the metric by what you're detecting: pass@k for collapse, sampled pass@1 for improvement.
- **Select training data by measured solvability** (learnability frontier), not difficulty label.
- SDPO on the frontier = **capability elicitation/sharpening** (pass@k→pass@1), bounded by base pass@k; new capability (hard) needs **live judge feedback** (iteration 2).
- **Select the frontier band by BINARY solve rate at the TRAINING temperature** (not a dense/coarse probe). The lever is *data selection* (which problems split under the binary advantage) — NOT dense reward (a distinct, gaming-prone alternative). iter-07's band (dense score, n=4 probe) was 69% flat-under-binary → `flat_group` 0.44; iter-08's n=12 binary re-probe (temp 1.0) → 10 problems → `flat_group` 0.12. Flat probability is `p⁸+(1−p)⁸`, minimized at p≈0.5; keep `p∈[~0.25,0.75]`.
- **Mode collapse here = a self-distillation diversity loss** (pass@k erodes; "length-held ≠ diversity-held" — pass@k is the metric, not length): flat reward groups → weak policy gradient → self-distillation sharpens. The **mechanism** is confirmed: frontier-band data revives the GRPO policy gradient (`flat_group` 0.75→0.44→0.12 across iter-06/07/08, a direct per-step measurement). **BUT reviving the gradient did NOT produce a measurable pass@8 gain** — the iter-08 definitive eval (30 problems, n=12, bootstrap CI) found ALL iterations' CIs overlap base. The earlier "+0.083 reversed the collapse, first iter to beat base" claim was **12-probe noise and is retracted**. **iter-09 CLOSED the open question:** applying the revived gradient at a real dose (LR 1e-4, ΔW→2.40) does NOT buy capability — it reproduces the iter-05 collapse (pass@1 −0.20, pass@8 −0.29, both paired-CI exclude 0; length 16k→3–8k). So: **weak dose → null, strong dose → collapse. The band revives the gradient but not a *useful direction*.** The fix is a direction lever (entropy/length guard, or KL — but `--beta` is inert in SDPOTrainer, `sdpo-beta-inert`), not just LR tuning.
- **Never let a small-probe Δ drive an iteration; gate every capability claim on a CI that excludes zero.** The whole iter-06/07/08 "collapse→fix" arc rested on a 12-problem × n=8 probe whose ±0.15 noise fabricated a ±0.25 "monotonic trend" that vanished on a 30-problem n=12 bootstrap-CI eval. A powered eval (≥30 problems, n≥12, bootstrap CI) run *once, early* is cheap insurance against chasing noise. pass@8 point estimates from <~25 problems are not a basis for any conclusion.
- **The 8B thinking-ON SDPO regime needs Modal H200 (≥141GB) — the GB10's 128GB unified memory OOM-cascades on the training backward** (full-vocab logits over ~20k-token sequences); the 512-cap smoke does NOT exercise that peak. GB10 stays the box for cheap diagnostics/eval/plotting.
- **Instrument runs to be reviewable** ([OBSERVABILITY.md](design/OBSERVABILITY.md)): persist rollout text + per-token logprobs + rich split metrics, or you can't diagnose a collapse after the fact (iter-05 saved none).
