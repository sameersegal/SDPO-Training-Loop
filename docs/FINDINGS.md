**Standing lessons (carry forward):**
- **pass@k (k‚â•4, n‚â•8) is the metric** ‚Äî greedy pass@1 (¬±2/25 noise) and the SDPO loss are both blind to regression.
- **Select training data by measured solvability** (learnability frontier), not difficulty label.
- SDPO on the frontier = **capability elicitation/sharpening** (pass@k‚Üípass@1), bounded by base pass@k; new capability (hard) needs **live judge feedback** (iteration 2).
- **Select the frontier band by BINARY solve rate at the TRAINING temperature** (not a dense/coarse probe). The lever is *data selection* (which problems split under the binary advantage) ‚Äî NOT dense reward (a distinct, gaming-prone alternative). iter-07's band (dense score, n=4 probe) was 69% flat-under-binary ‚Üí `flat_group` 0.44; iter-08's n=12 binary re-probe (temp 1.0) ‚Üí 10 problems ‚Üí `flat_group` 0.12. Flat probability is `p‚Å∏+(1‚àíp)‚Å∏`, minimized at p‚âà0.5; keep `p‚àà[~0.25,0.75]`.
- **Mode collapse here = a self-distillation diversity loss** (pass@k erodes; "length-held ‚âÝ diversity-held" ‚Äî pass@k is the metric, not length): flat reward groups ‚Üí weak policy gradient ‚Üí self-distillation sharpens. The **mechanism** is confirmed: frontier-band data revives the GRPO policy gradient (`flat_group` 0.75‚Üí0.44‚Üí0.12 across iter-06/07/08, a direct per-step measurement). **BUT reviving the gradient did NOT produce a measurable pass@8 gain** ‚Äî the iter-08 definitive eval (30 problems, n=12, bootstrap CI) found ALL iterations' CIs overlap base. The earlier "+0.083 reversed the collapse, first iter to beat base" claim was **12-probe noise and is retracted**. Open: whether more dose (long run) + a powered eval reveals a real effect. (KL-to-base is *not* an available lever ‚Äî `--beta` is inert in SDPOTrainer, `sdpo-beta-inert`.)
- **Never let a small-probe Œî drive an iteration; gate every capability claim on a CI that excludes zero.** The whole iter-06/07/08 "collapse‚Üífix" arc rested on a 12-problem √ó n=8 probe whose ¬±0.15 noise fabricated a ¬±0.25 "monotonic trend" that vanished on a 30-problem n=12 bootstrap-CI eval. A powered eval (‚â•30 problems, n‚â•12, bootstrap CI) run *once, early* is cheap insurance against chasing noise. pass@8 point estimates from <~25 problems are not a basis for any conclusion.
- **The 8B thinking-ON SDPO regime needs Modal H200 (‚â•141GB) ‚Äî the GB10's 128GB unified memory OOM-cascades on the training backward** (full-vocab logits over ~20k-token sequences); the 512-cap smoke does NOT exercise that peak. GB10 stays the box for cheap diagnostics/eval/plotting.
- **Instrument runs to be reviewable** ([OBSERVABILITY.md](design/OBSERVABILITY.md)): persist rollout text + per-token logprobs + rich split metrics, or you can't diagnose a collapse after the fact (iter-05 saved none).
- **Spend tracking:** cross-iteration cost segmentation (Train / Eval / Infra / **Not productive**) lives in [`reports/comparison/SPEND.md`](../reports/comparison/SPEND.md) ‚Äî **~35% of all Modal spend was wasted compute**, almost all of it the pre-hardening failure modes (iter-03 integration false-starts, iter-05 eval crash/retry).
